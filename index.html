How to Drive a Car: A Step by Step Guide


         

When an individual is learning how to drive a car, there are several things that they should keep in mind. Regardless of whether one is a seasoned or rookie driver, it is imperative to be aware of certain aspects when one is behind the steering wheel.
         Several individuals know how to drive an automatic car but are not comfortable behind the wheels of a manual one. The opposite holds true as well. Therefore, to help out all individuals, this article will illustrate all vital aspects of driving both these types of cars.



What Are the Things to Do Before Driving a Car for the First Time?

         

Individuals searching on how to drive a car for beginners must first ensure to do the following things before starting to drive:

1:Adjust driver seat and mirrors as per preference.
         2:Put on a seatbelt and engage the parking or emergency brake.
3:Get familiarised with the gear shift pattern mentioned on the gear-lever and try a mock gear change to get the hang of it.
         4:Besides this, one must also ensure that they are on a level field to avoid rolling back or ahead.
  & 5:After doing all of these, individuals looking for answers on how to drive a car in India, can proceed with the steps to drive a manual and automatic car.


How to Drive a Manual Car for the First Time?



Follow these steps if you are looking for how to drive a manual car for beginners:

Step 1: Start a Car in Neutral

      Start the vehicle in neutral, turn on the ignition key to crank the engine and depress the clutch pedal. Drivers might not have to depress the clutch for switching on an older car model, but most modern cars are engineered in a way that it is necessary to engage the clutch before starting the engine.
      This practice also ensures that a vehicle will not give any hiccups while starting, even if a driver forgets to put the gearbox in neutral.

Step 2: Shift into First Gear

      With the engine running, drivers must engage the clutch entirely with their left foot and shift the gear-lever into first gear. At this point, individuals must use the left foot only for operating the clutch and the right foot for modulating brake pedals and accelerator.

Step 3: Get the Car in Motion

       To set a car in motion, drivers must disengage the parking brake while keeping the clutch pedal depressed. They must not accelerate at this point. Now, drivers should start lifting the clutch pedal until they notice a drop in revs and clutch plates start coming in contact. This is also known as ‚Äòbite point‚Äô. Individuals searching on how to drive a car, should note that disengaging the clutch further at this point will make the vehicle crawl ahead without any accelerator input.

Step 4: Upshift into Higher Gears

       Now that the car is in motion, release the accelerator and push down on the clutch. Then by following the shift pattern, change the gear-lever to second gear. After upshifting, disengage the clutch and begin applying moderate throttle inputs for maintaining momentum. Individuals looking for how to drive a manual car must note that this entire procedure of engaging and releasing the clutch, modulating throttle inputs, etc. needs to be synchronised in quick succession.

Step 5: Downshift to Lower Gears

       A car tends to shudder when stuck in a traffic jam and starts slowing down. In such cases, drivers must downshift a certain number of gears by following the same procedure - release the accelerator, depress the clutch and shift down to a lower gear.

Step 6: Stop a Manual Car

       To completely stop a manual car, disengage the accelerator and apply moderate force on the brake pedal to gradually downshift the gears to first gear. Once the vehicle is moving at a crawling speed, shift to neutral and allow it to freewheel without the involvement of any throttle input. Then allow the vehicle to come to a halt by engaging the clutch and depressing the brake completely.

Wondering what to do during an emergency situation?

       While searching on how to drive a car, individuals must note that they can stop a vehicle in any gear during an emergency situation by pushing down on their vehicle‚Äôs clutch and shifting it to neutral.

Step 7: Reversing a Manual Car

       Firstly, beginners having a search query on how to drive a car must ensure their vehicle is at a complete stop before shifting it to reverse gear. While reversing, they must check out that there is no vehicle or individual behind their vehicle before backing up.

Step 8: Parking a Manual Car

       Unlike an automatic car, a manual one does not come with a ‚ÄòPark‚Äô gear. Therefore, it is imperative to engage a parking or emergency brake every time while parking a vehicle. Alternatively, drivers can also shift their vehicle into the reverse or first gear to prevent it from moving or rolling.
